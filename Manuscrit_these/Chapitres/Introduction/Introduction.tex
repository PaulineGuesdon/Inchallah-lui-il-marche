\subsection{Transverse cooling} 
There are then collimated by Transverse Cooling (TC) stationnary waves that are propagating perpendicular to the atomic flux: this way the 
transverse velocity of the atoms is reduced by recoil energy $\hbar k_{\perp}$ with $k_{\perp}$ the transverse component of the TC electromagnetic
wavevector. They mostly propagate along the axis of the coils there for Zeeman cooling. 
The beam comes from a master-slave system (see Fig \ref{fig:intro-chaine-bleue}) where the slave is a cavity with a gain medium that 
delivers more power after passing through it. The optical frequency of this laser is set at $f_{TC} = f_{Blue~Master} + f^{RF}_{TC}$ 
with $f^{RF}_{TC} = $ the modulating frequency of the "TC AOM". \\
The frequency is red detuned from the targeted $^1S_0-~^3P_1$ transition: because the temperature is high the atoms have multiple 
velocity classes. By Doppler shift atoms of different velocities have only a red detuned frequency shift then the laser with a broad 
spectrum is resonant with atoms of different velocities as illustrate \ref{doppler shift}.

\subsection{Blue MOT}
\subsubsection{The physics}
The Magnetic Optical Trap (MOT) purpose is to trap the atoms in a small volume to cool them down. The first step of cooling is to do a blue MOT 
that uses the $^1S_0~-~^1P_1~,F=$ transition first to coincide the broad resonance with the broad Doppler shift velocity distribution.
This MOT is composed by a magnetic field gradient created by pairs of coils in the three directions, centered in the middle of a vacuum chamber and combined
to the same laser as before at $461$~nm. There is the exact same behavior in each dimension which is why - for simplicity - I will describe the phenomenon along
x only. 

One start with a small gradient at $B = ~G$ on atoms that are spread in a wide volume because of their kinetic temperature. This field expresses as $B \propto x$ 
which means that the fthe degeneracy lift $\Delta E = -m_F g \mu_B B$ is higher at extreme positions. 

It is combined with a counterpropagating beam that passes through a $\lambda /4$ waveplate to turn its polarization it's opposite circular. The interaction between 
light and matter takes the form of the radiation pressure that pushes the atoms with high velocity to the center of the B field: the more the velocity the stronger 
is this restoring force. At this level the laser is at a fixed frequency and the multiple spin projections are shifted also proportionnal to the position because 
the B field is linear with the position. Then when an atom is at position $a$ the laser is resonant with $m_F = -9/2$ and the atom looses $\hbar k$ energy that takes 
him close to the center. It becomes necessary to increase the gradient since the atoms are tending to smaller mean velocity closer to the $0^{th}$ position. 

The temperature of the gas attempt Doppler limit $k_B T = \frac{\hbar ~\Gamma_{^1S_0-^1P_1}}{2}$ that is colder than the recoil temperature.

\subsection{Raman transitions}
\subsubsection{Physical concept}
The second chapter of this thesis is about spin manipulation on SU(10) manifold. This operation is done by two-photons Raman process from two different lasers adressing
with a red detuning from the intercombination line. 

One want to control the size of the Hilbert space from one to ten which necessits the independant manipulation of the spin states. Raman transition on this line 
is the coupling of an $\ket{F, m_F}$ ground state manifold to a state from an ensemble of hyperfine states which has the form

\begin{equation}
    \hbar \Omega = E \times E^* \sum_{0}^{F = n} \frac{\bra{F, m_F'}\vec{d}.\vec{E}\ket{F, m_F}\bra{F, m_F}\vec{d}.\vec{E}\ket{F, m_F'}}{\delta}
\end{equation}

The energy difference between two consecutive states is simply 
\begin{equation}
    \Delta E_{m_F}^{m_{F+1}} = q(2 m_F +1) + \beta
\end{equation}

that shows the unique value of the energy difference between two consecutive states.

\subsection{Photoassociation}
The third chapter is about photoassociating atoms under the intercombination line. The beam used for this effect is tuned by the same 'AOM lattice 689' with all the power 
injected in this AOM instead of shared with the 'Raman AOM'. For all the datas of chapter 3 this optical chain is composed by a homemade confocal Fabry-Perot cavity locked by a 
Pound-Drever-Hall locking system (PDH) demonstrated in \cite{black_introduction_2001}, inserted in the previous scheme for Raman process giving the final scheme of Fig.\ref{raman transitions}.\\

The distance between the two curved mirrors is set for all the spacial electric modes to be resonant at the same frequency. It enables to maximize the transmitted intensity of the laser
through the mirrors of reflectivity R = 99$\%$. This cavity has a final transmission of $T = 0.54$ when locked on the laser for a Free Spectral Range
defined as $FSR = \frac{c}{2L} = 3~$GHz.
