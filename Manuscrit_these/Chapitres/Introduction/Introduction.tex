\section{Fermi gas preparation (+ Bose gas ?)}
The strontium is transformed from solid to gas form by an oven at 490°C through multiple small capillaries in a collimation purpose.  

\subsection{TC} 
There are then collimated by Transverse Cooling stationnary waves that are propagating perpendicular to the atomic flux: this way the 
transverse velocity of the atoms is reduced so they mostly propagate along the axis of the coils there for Zeeman cooling. 
The beam comes from a master-slave system (see Fig \ref{fig:intro-chaine-bleue}) where the slave is a cavity with a gain medium that 
delivers more power after passing through it. The optical frequency of this laser is set at $f_{TC} = f_{Blue~Master} + f^{RF}_{TC}$ 
with $f^{RF}_{TC} = $ the modulating frequency of the "TC AOM". \\
The frequency is red detuned from the targeted $^1S_0~^3P_1$ transition: by Doppler shift more velocity classes of atoms are affected 
by the TC.
